Project 0

Becoming Friends with Your Camera

The goal of this project is to gain some intuitive understanding of the somewhat subtle relationship between perspective, focal length/zoom, and the center of projection. Here are some photos I took to illustrate these concepts.

Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Portrait @ 5cm Away

Portrait @ 2m Away

Say hello to my friend Kai! Here he is at two very different perspectives. When taking a selfie, you may have noticed yourself that you look more natural when the camera is further away. This is because cameras require a larger field of view or shorter focal length to capture the same frame when up close.

Architectural Perspective Compression

Wurster Hall (Close - No Zoom)

Wurster Hall (Far - Zoom)

These images capture Wurster from contrasting perespectives. Similar to the portraits, the close up makes the depth of the image more tangible. The zoomed in image from far away should appear more compressed. The image is more "flat" because depth is less apparent.

The Dolly Zoom

Animation Sequence

Individual Frame Inspection

The dolly zoom effect is a cinematic technique that creates a disorienting visual effect by simultaneously moving the camera forward or backward while zooming in or out to keep the subject the same scale. This creates a unique perspective where the subject appears to stay the same size while the background dramatically changes, producing a sense of vertigo or unease. This is also named the "Vertigo shot" after the famous scene in Hitchcock's Vertigo.

In this sequence, you can see how the background around the Pappy statue changes while maintaining the same/similar framing of the subject. This makes the trees in the background warp or distort inward while the field of view expands behind Pappy.
